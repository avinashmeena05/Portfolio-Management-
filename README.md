# 📈 Dynamic Portfolio Management & Asset Allocation Strategy

## Overview

Designed an end-to-end portfolio management framework simulating the role of an investment manager responsible for constructing and managing a multi-asset portfolio over an 8-year investment horizon.

The objective was to optimize wealth creation while satisfying future liquidity requirements through fundamental equity research, dynamic asset allocation, macroeconomic scenario analysis, and risk-adjusted portfolio optimization.

The portfolio successfully scaled an initial **$94K investment corpus to ~$369K**, generating an **18.63% CAGR**, while fulfilling an intermediate **$59K liquidity requirement**.

---

## Portfolio Objective

- Initial Corpus: **$94K**
- Investment Horizon: **8 Years (2025–2033)**
- Target Corpus: **~$362K**
- Final Portfolio Value: **~$369K**
- Required CAGR: **18.35%**
- Achieved CAGR: **18.63%**

---

## Investment Framework

### 1. Fundamental Equity Screening

Analyzed **15+ companies across 10+ sectors** using a multi-factor investment framework:

- Revenue Growth (Sales CAGR)
- Profit CAGR
- Return on Equity (ROE)
- Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)
- Debt-to-Equity Ratio
- Historical Stock Performance

Companies were scored based on financial strength, growth potential, and long-term sustainability.

---

## Expected Return Forecasting Model

Developed a Growth Sustainability Adjustment framework:

Expected CAGR = Historical CAGR × Adjustment Factor

Adjustment factors were determined based on:

- Industry growth outlook
- Competitive positioning
- Company maturity
- Valuation sustainability
- Future growth drivers

This allowed historical performance to be converted into realistic forward-looking return expectations.

### Phase 2: Opportunity Capture Strategy (2028–2031)

Adapted allocation based on changing macroeconomic conditions:

- EV ecosystem expansion
- Manufacturing growth

Portfolio exposure was adjusted toward sectors benefiting from structural growth.

### Phase 3: Wealth Preservation Strategy (2031–2033)

Transitioned portfolio towards:

- Defensive sectors
